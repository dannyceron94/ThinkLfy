Food

Every living animal takes in food, metabolizes it to extract energy and nutrients, and discharges wastes. Almost all of the food for humans comes either from agriculture or from harvesting animals from the oceans. Currently agriculture occupies about 35 percent of the Earth’s land surface (Wijkman and Rockström 2012, 52).

green revolution introduced the technology of industrial agriculture, with chemical fertilizers, pesticides, irrigation, and hybrid seeds. While the green revolution improved yields and ended the specter of mass famines, it came with environmental costs, and it did not end the problem of hunger. Since the green revolution, food production in developing countries has increased, doubling or tripling in many places. However, because population has also nearly doubled, the amount of food produced per person has remained nearly unchanged in most parts of the world.
