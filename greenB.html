Green Buildings

what is a green building?

The term “green building” is used to describe a healthy building that minimizes negative impacts on the environment. It uses the free sources of energy and water that are available around it, particularly sunlight, wind, and rain. Like a living organism, it is adapted to the specific conditions in the particular place where it is.

Green buildings designs strive to reduce or eliminate greenhouse gas emissions, reduce energy use, minimize or eliminate dependence on fossil fuels, reduce water use, reduce waste, avoid pollution, protect natural resources, preserve or restore local 225 ecosystems, provide places that are comfortable and healthy for people, and build community. Designers integrate multiple strategies for meeting these goals.

Becoming one

The process to design of a green building often begins with a charrette. fast-paced, highly collaborative, carefully orchestrated method for exchanging and building upon ideas, common in community planning as well as building design. It is common for the design charrette process to begin by referring to the LEED checklistt. LEED provides a way to organize the thinking about goals and strategies. Once building systems have been designed and constructed or installed, it is important to verify that they are functioning as intended. This is done through the process called building commissioning.
